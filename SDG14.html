SDG 14: Life Below Water

Conserve and sustainable use the oceans, seas, and marine resources

What is SDG 14?

SDG 14, also known as "Life Below Water", is one of the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. It focuses on protecting oceans, seas, and marine life by reducing pollution, preventing overfishing, and conserving the marine ecosystem.

Oceans are also very important because they produce more than half of the oxygen we breathe and help control the Earth's climate. They also provide food and jobs for billions of people worldwide.

Why is SDG 14 Important?

Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and are home to millions of species. They play a key role in keeping our planet healthy. However, pollution, climate change and overfishing are damaging marine life.

Plastic waster, oil spills and the rising of the ocean temperatures are destroying habitats like coral reefs and threatening many marine animals. Protecting oceans help protect our future.
